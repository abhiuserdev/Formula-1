📊 Periodic Trends (P-Block Focus)

▼

Atomic Radius Trend

Increases down the group; decreases across the period. Noble gases have largest van der Waals radius.

Ionic Radius

Cation < Neutral Atom < Anion. Isoelectronic species: radius $$\propto \frac{1}{Z_{eff}}$$

Ionization Energy Order (Group 15 vs 16)

$$N > O$$, $$P > S$$, $$As > Se$$ (Half-filled p³ stability in group 15)

Ionization Energy Order (Group 13 vs 14)

$$B > Al$$, $$Ga > Al$$, $$Ga > In$$ (d-block contraction effect on Ga)

Electronegativity Trend

Increases across period, decreases down group. Pauling scale: F > O > Cl > N > Br

Electron Affinity Exceptions

Group 15 (half-filled) has lower EA. Cl > F (F has small size, interelectronic repulsion)

Inert Pair Effect

Stability of ns² electrons increases down the group. Heavier elements show +1, +3 instead of +3, +5.

Inert Pair Effect Order

$$Tl⁺ > In⁺ > Ga⁺$$ and $$Pb²⁺ > Sn²⁺ > Ge²⁺$$

Oxidation States (Group 13)

+3 most stable. +1 becomes more stable down the group due to inert pair effect (Tl⁺ > Tl³⁺)

Oxidation States (Group 14)

+4 most stable for C, Si, Ge, Sn. +2 more stable for Pb (inert pair effect)

Oxidation States (Group 15)

+5 most stable for N, P. +3 more stable for Bi. N shows max covalency of 4 (no d-orbitals)

Oxidation States (Group 16/17)

Oxygen shows only -2, -1 (peroxide), except OF₂ (+2). Halogens: Cl, Br, I show +1 to +7.

Catenation Trend

C >> Si > Ge ≈ Sn. Decreases down group due to decreasing M-M bond energy.

Allotropy

C: diamond, graphite, fullerene, graphene. S: rhombic, monoclinic, plastic. P: white, red, black.

Acidic Nature of Oxides

Increases with oxidation state and electronegativity. $$Cl₂O₇ > Cl₂O₅ > Cl₂O₃ > Cl₂O$$

Acidic/Basic Nature (Down Group)

Basic character of oxides increases down group. Acidic character decreases down group.

Bond Dissociation Enthalpy (H-X)

$$H-F > H-Cl < H-Br < H-I$$. HF has highest due to small size; H-Cl lower due to orbital mismatch.

Bond Energy (X-X)

$$Cl-Cl > Br-Br > F-F > I-I$$. F-F is weak due to lone pair repulsion.

Oxidizing Power of Halogens

$$F₂ > Cl₂ > Br₂ > I₂$$. Depends on electron affinity, hydration energy, bond dissociation.

Reducing Power of Hydrides

$$NH₃ < PH₃ < AsH₃ < SbH₃ < BiH₃$$ (increases down group, bond strength decreases)

Thermal Stability of Hydrides

$$NH₃ > PH₃ > AsH₃ > SbH₃ > BiH₃$$. Decreases down group (M-H bond energy decreases).

Basicity of Hydrides (Group 15)

$$NH₃ > PH₃ > AsH₃ > SbH₃$$. Decreases down group; lone pair availability decreases.

Acidity of Hydrides (Group 16/17)

$$H₂O < H₂S < H₂Se < H₂Te$$ and $$HF < HCl < HBr < HI$$. Increases down group.

Melting Point Anomalies

Ga has mp 29.76°C (just above room temp). Bi has lower mp than Sb (metallic bonding weakens).

Boiling Point Trend (Group 15/16/17)

NH₃, H₂O, HF have anomalously high bp due to H-bonding. Order: H₂O > HF > NH₃.

Metallic Character

Increases down group. Graphite is non-metallic conductor. Sn, Pb are metals.

Polarizability & Fajan's Rules

Cation: small size, high charge → high polarizing power. Anion: large size, high charge → more polarizable.

Coordination Number Trend

Maximum CN = 4 for N (no d-orbitals). Si, P, S can expand octet using d-orbitals (CN up to 6).

Availability of d-orbitals

N cannot form $$NCl₅$$ (no d-orbitals). P forms $$PCl₅$$ (uses 3d). This is key for JEE!
