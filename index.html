Radial nodes = $$n - l - 1$$. Angular nodes = $$l$$. Total nodes = $$n - 1$$

📘 Ch 3: Classification of Elements

▼

Periodic Trends

Radius: ↓ across period, ↑ down group. IE: ↑ across, ↓ down (exceptions: Group 15, d-block contraction)

Effective Nuclear Charge

$$Z_{eff} = Z - \sigma$$ (Slater's rules). Shielding: s > p > d > f for same n

Diagonal Relationship

Li ↔ Mg, Be ↔ Al, B ↔ Si. Similar ionic size/polarizing power cause similar properties.

Electronegativity Scales

Pauling: based on bond energies. Mulliken: $$\frac{IE + EA}{2}$$. Alfred-Rochow: based on effective nuclear charge

📘 Ch 4: Chemical Bonding

▼

VSEPR & Hybridization

$$AX_2$$: sp, linear. $$AX_3$$: sp², trigonal planar. $$AX_4$$: sp³, tetrahedral. $$AX_5$$: sp³d. $$AX_6$$: sp³d²

Dipole Moment

$$\mu = q \times d$$. % ionic character = $$\frac{\mu_{obs}}{\mu_{ionic}} \times 100$$. Symmetric molecules: μ = 0

Bond Order (MOT)

$$BO = \frac{N_b - N_a}{2}$$. If BO > 0, molecule stable. Higher BO → shorter bond, higher BE

Fajan's Rules

Cation polarizing power ↑ with charge ↑ and size ↓. Anion polarizability ↑ with size ↑ and charge ↑

Hydrogen Bonding

Requires H bonded to F, O, or N. Intermolecular H-bonding ↑ bp (H₂O, HF, NH₃). Intramolecular ↓ bp (o-nitrophenol)

📘 Ch 5: States of Matter

▼

Gas Laws

$$PV = nRT$$. $$\frac{P_1V_1}{T_1} = \frac{P_2V_2}{T_2}$$. $$d = \frac{PM}{RT}$$. Dalton: $$P_{total} = \sum P_i$$

Kinetic Theory

$$KE_{avg} = \frac{3}{2}k_BT$$ per molecule. $$u_{rms} = \sqrt{\frac{3RT}{M}}$$. Graham: $$\frac{r_1}{r_2} = \sqrt{\frac{M_2}{M_1}}$$

van der Waals

$$\left(P + \frac{an^2}{V^2}\right)(V - nb) = nRT$$. $$a$$: intermolecular attraction. $$b$$: co-volume (excluded volume)

Compressibility Factor

$$Z = \frac{PV}{nRT}$$. Ideal gas: Z = 1. Real gas: Z ≠ 1. At low P: Z = 1 - a/RTV

📘 Ch 6: Thermodynamics

▼

First Law

$$\Delta U = q + w$$ (IUPAC: w = work done ON system). $$\Delta H = \Delta U + \Delta n_g RT$$

Hess's Law

$$\Delta H_{total} = \sum \Delta H_{steps}$$. Independent of path. Used to find enthalpies of formation.

Entropy & Gibbs Energy

$$\Delta S = \frac{q_{rev}}{T}$$. $$\Delta G = \Delta H - T\Delta S$$. Spontaneous if $$\Delta G < 0$$

Standard Enthalpies

Formation: $$\Delta H_f°$$ (elements in standard state = 0). Combustion: $$\Delta H_c°$$. Neutralization: $$-57.1$$ kJ/mol (strong acid+base)

Bond Enthalpy

$$\Delta H = \sum BE_{reactants} - \sum BE_{products}$$. Average bond enthalpy for diatomic = exact

📘 Ch 7: Equilibrium

▼

Equilibrium Constant

$$K_p = K_c(RT)^{\Delta n}$$. Pure solids/liquids omitted. $$\Delta n = \text{moles gaseous products} - \text{moles gaseous reactants}$$

Reaction Quotient

$$Q_c = \frac{[Products]}{[Reactants]}$$. If Q < K: forward. Q > K: backward. Q = K: equilibrium.

Le Chatelier's Principle

System opposes change: ↑ concentration → shifts to consume it. ↑ pressure → shifts to fewer moles. ↑ T → shifts to endothermic side.

pH & pOH

$$pH = -\log[H^+]$$, $$pOH = -\log[OH^-]$$. $$pH + pOH = 14$$ (at 25°C). $$K_w = 10^{-14}$$

Weak Acid & Base

$$K_a = \frac{C\alpha^2}{1-\alpha} \approx C\alpha^2$$ (if α << 1). $$[H^+] = \sqrt{K_a \cdot C}$$. $$pH = \frac{1}{2}(pK_a - \log C)$$

Hydrolysis & Buffer

$$pH = 7 + \frac{1}{2}(pK_a + \log C)$$ (salt of WA + SB). Henderson: $$pH = pK_a + \log\frac{[salt]}{[acid]}$$

📘 Ch 8: Redox Reactions

▼

Oxidation Number Rules

O = -2 (peroxide: -1, OF₂: +2). H = +1 (metal hydrides: -1). Group 1 = +1, Group 2 = +2. F = -1 always.

Equivalent Weight

$$E = \frac{M}{n}$$ where n = no. of e⁻ transferred. Normality = n × Molarity

Balancing Redox

Ion-electron method: balance atoms, then O (using H₂O), then H (using H⁺), then charge (using e⁻). Acidic medium: H⁺, H₂O. Basic: OH⁻, H₂O

📘 Ch 10: s-Block Elements

▼

Trends & Anomalies

IE: Li > Na > K > Rb > Cs. Be > Mg > Ca > Sr > Ba. Hydration energy: Li⁺ > Na⁺ > K⁺ (small size)

Solubility

Hydroxides: solubility increases down group. Carbonates/sulphates: solubility decreases down group (except Be).

Flame Test Colors

Li: crimson red, Na: yellow, K: lilac, Ca: brick red, Sr: crimson, Ba: apple green

Important Compounds

Plaster of Paris: CaSO₄·½H₂O. Dead burnt plaster: CaSO₄. Epsom salt: MgSO₄·7H₂O. Sorel's cement: MgCl₂ + MgO

📘 Ch 11: p-Block (Groups 13-14)

▼

Boron & Aluminium

B(OH)₃ is acidic (Lewis acid). AlCl₃ dimerizes in non-polar solvents. Al forms [AlF₆]³⁻ but B forms [BF₄]⁻ only.

Inert Pair Effect

Stability of ns² electrons increases down group. Tl⁺ > Tl³⁺, Pb²⁺ > Pb⁴⁺. Ga > Al in IE (d-block contraction)

Carbon & Silicon

Catenation: C >> Si > Ge. CO is neutral oxide, CO₂ acidic. SiO₂ is network solid (3D). Silicates: ortho SiO₄⁴⁻, pyro Si₂O₇⁶⁻

Important Reactions

Borax bead test for metal ions. Al in aq. NaOH: 2Al + 2NaOH + 2H₂O → 2NaAlO₂ + 3H₂. Silicates form zeolites (use as molecular sieves)

📗 Ch 12-1: Solid State

▼

Packing Efficiency

Simple cubic: 52.4%. BCC: 68%. FCC/HCP: 74%. Coordination numbers: SC=6, BCC=8, FCC=12

Density of Crystal

$$d = \frac{Z \times M}{a^3 \times N_A}$$. Z = atoms per unit cell. For FCC: Z=4, BCC: Z=2, SC: Z=1

Radius Relations

SC: $$r = \frac{a}{2}$$. BCC: $$r = \frac{\sqrt{3}a}{4}$$. FCC: $$r = \frac{a}{2\sqrt{2}}$$

Voids

Tetrahedral voids = 2N, Octahedral voids = N. Radius ratio: tetrahedral = 0.225, octahedral = 0.414

Bragg's Equation

$$n\lambda = 2d\sin\theta$$. Used to determine crystal structure by X-ray diffraction.

📗 Ch 12-2: Solutions

▼

Raoult's Law

For volatile: $$P = x_1P_1° + x_2P_2°$$. For non-volatile solute: $$\frac{P°-P}{P°} = x_2$$ (relative lowering of VP)

Colligative Properties

$$\Delta T_b = iK_b m$$, $$\Delta T_f = iK_f m$$, $$\pi = iMRT$$. van't Hoff factor: $$i = \frac{\text{observed value}}{\text{normal value}}$$

Molar Mass from Colligative

$$M_2 = \frac{K_b \times w_2 \times 1000}{\Delta T_b \times w_1}$$. For dissociation: $$i = 1 + (n-1)\alpha$$. Association: $$i = 1 - \left(1-\frac{1}{n}\right)\alpha$$

Henry's Law

$$p = K_H \cdot x$$. Higher $$K_H$$ → lower gas solubility. Valid at moderate pressure and low temperature.

📗 Ch 12-3: Electrochemistry

▼

Nernst Equation

$$E = E° - \frac{RT}{nF}\ln Q = E° - \frac{0.0591}{n}\log Q$$ (at 298K)

Cell EMF & Gibbs

$$E°_{cell} = E°_{cathode} - E°_{anode}$$. $$\Delta G = -nFE$$. $$\Delta G° = -nFE° = -RT\ln K$$

Conductivity

$$\Lambda_m = \frac{\kappa \times 1000}{M}$$, $$\Lambda_{eq} = \frac{\kappa \times 1000}{N}$$. Kohlrausch: $$\Lambda°_m = \nu_+\lambda°_+ + \nu_-\lambda°_-$$

Degree of Dissociation

$$\alpha = \frac{\Lambda_m}{\Lambda°_m}$$. Ostwald: $$K_a = \frac{C\alpha^2}{1-\alpha}$$. For weak electrolyte at infinite dilution: $$\alpha \to 1$$

Faraday's Laws

$$m = ZIt = \frac{MIt}{nF}$$. $$1F = 96500$$ C. Equivalent weight = Molar mass/n-factor

📗 Ch 12-4: Chemical Kinetics

▼

Rate Laws

Zero order: $$[A] = [A]_0 - kt$$, $$t_{1/2} = \frac{[A]_0}{2k}$$. First order: $$\ln[A] = \ln[A]_0 - kt$$, $$t_{1/2} = \frac{0.693}{k}$$

Second Order

$$\frac{1}{[A]} = \frac{1}{[A]_0} + kt$$. $$t_{1/2} = \frac{1}{k[A]_0}$$. Units: zero=M/s, first=s⁻¹, second=M⁻¹s⁻¹

Arrhenius Equation

$$k = Ae^{-E_a/RT}$$. $$\ln\frac{k_2}{k_1} = \frac{E_a}{R}\left(\frac{1}{T_1} - \frac{1}{T_2}\right)$$

Molecularity vs Order

Molecularity = number of reactant molecules in elementary step. Order = experimentally determined exponents sum.

Half-Life Relationships

Zero order: $$t_{1/2} \propto [A]_0$$. First order: $$t_{1/2}$$ independent. Second order: $$t_{1/2} \propto \frac{1}{[A]_0}$$

📗 Ch 12-7: p-Block (Groups 15-18)

▼

Nitrogen Family

NH₃: strongest base (gas phase). HNO₃ is strong oxidizing agent. P₄ + NaOH → PH₃ + NaH₂PO₂. PCl₃: pyramidal, PCl₅: trigonal bipyramidal

Oxygen Family

H₂SO₄: contact process (V₂O₅ catalyst). Oleum: H₂S₂O₇. H₂O₂: both oxidant and reductant. Ozone: diamagnetic, bent structure

Halogens

Oxidizing power: F₂ > Cl₂ > Br₂ > I₂. HF weakest acid (H-bonding). ICl, IBr are interhalogen compounds. Xenon forms XeF₂, XeF₄, XeF₆, XeO₃, XeO₄

Noble Gases

He: lowest bp. Rn: radioactive. Xe compounds possible due to low IE and large size. XeF₂ linear, XeF₄ square planar, XeF₆ distorted octahedral

📗 Ch 12-8: d & f Block

▼

Lanthanide Contraction

Steady decrease in atomic/ionic size from La to Lu due to poor shielding by 4f e⁻. Consequences: Zr≈Hf, Nb≈Ta, Mo≈W

Transition Metal Properties

Variable oxidation states due to comparable (n-1)d and ns energy. Color due to d-d transitions. Catalytic property due to variable oxidation states and surface area

Important Compounds

KMnO₄: strong oxidant in acidic medium (Mn²⁺), neutral (MnO₂), alkaline (MnO₄²⁻). K₂Cr₂O₇: orange, strong oxidant in acidic medium (Cr³⁺)

Magnetic Moment

Spin only: $$\mu = \sqrt{n(n+2)}$$ BM. If observed > calculated: orbital contribution present.

📗 Ch 12-9: Coordination Compounds

▼

Werner's Theory

Primary valency = oxidation state (ionizable). Secondary valency = coordination number (non-ionizable, directional)

EAN Rule

$$EAN = Z - x + 2n$$. For stability, EAN = 36 (Kr), 54 (Xe), 86 (Rn). Example: [Fe(CN)₆]⁴⁻ EAN = 26 - 2 + 12 = 36

Crystal Field Theory

Octahedral: $$\Delta_o$$. Tetrahedral: $$\Delta_t = \frac{4}{9}\Delta_o$$. Strong field (CN⁻, CO, NO₂⁻): low spin. Weak field (H₂O, F⁻, Cl⁻): high spin

Isomerism

[Ma₃b₃]: fac and mer. [M(AA)₂b₂]: cis-trans, optically active only for cis. [Mabcd]: optical isomerism in tetrahedral

📗 Ch 12-10 to 13: Organic Reactions

▼

SN1 vs SN2

SN1: 3° > 2° > 1°, carbocation, polar protic solvent, two steps. SN2: 1° > 2° > 3°, inversion, polar aprotic, one step

Elimination vs Substitution

E1: 3° > 2°, carbocation. E2: anti-periplanar geometry. E1cb: with β-carbonyl. Bulky base favors elimination

Carbonyl Reactions

Aldehydes more reactive than ketones (steric + electronic). Tollens': Ag⁺ → Ag mirror (aldehydes only). Fehling's: Cu²⁺ → Cu₂O red (aliphatic aldehydes)

Amines Basicity

Gas phase: 2° > 3° > 1° > NH₃. Aqueous: 2° > 1° > 3° > NH₃ (solvation effect). Aniline < NH₃ (resonance delocalization of lone pair)

Tests & Reagents

2,4-DNP: test for carbonyls. Schiff's base: pink for aldehydes. Carbylamine: 1° amine → foul smell. Hinsberg: distinguishes 1°, 2°, 3° amines
